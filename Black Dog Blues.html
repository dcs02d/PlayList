Black Dog Blues

Recorded by Blind Blake in 1927

Arthur Blake (1896 - December 1, 1934), known as Blind Blake, was an American blues and ragtime singer and guitarist. He is known for numerous recordings he made for Paramount Records between 1926 and 1932.

Big Bill Broonzy thought that he had frozen to death after falling over drunk during a Chicago blizzard and was too overweight to get back up.

OR In April 1933 he was hospitalized with pneumonia and never fully recovered. The cause of death was listed as pulmonary tuberculosis. He was buried in Glen Oaks Cemetery, in Glendale, Wisconsin in an unmarked grave.
